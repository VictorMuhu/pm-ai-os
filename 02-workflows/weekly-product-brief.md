# AI Weekly Product Brief

## Purpose

The Weekly Product Brief summarizes key signals from product usage, customer feedback, and experimentation.

It helps product managers quickly understand what is happening and decide what actions to take.

---

## Customer Signals

**Sources:**
- support tickets
- customer interviews
- Slack discussions
- research insights

**Output should summarize:**
- customer themes
- frequency of each theme
- business or user impact

**Example:**

| Theme | Evidence | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding friction | 34 support tickets | Activation risk |

---

## Metrics Review

Summarize key product metrics for the week.

**Focus areas:**
- activation
- retention
- engagement
- revenue

**Highlight:**
- meaningful metric movement week over week
- unexpected trends that need investigation

---

## Experiment Results

Review recent product experiments.

For each experiment include:

- **Hypothesis** — what was being tested
- **Result** — what the data showed
- **Interpretation** — what it means for the product

---

## Risks

Identify potential product risks this week:

- customer dissatisfaction signals
- technical risk or reliability issues
- metric degradation trends
- operational issues affecting product delivery

---

## Recommended Actions

Based on the signals above, recommend specific next steps:

- **Investigate** — signals that need more diagnosis before acting
- **Product improvements** — changes worth prioritizing
- **Experiments** — hypotheses worth testing
- **Communication updates** — stakeholders who need to be informed

---

## Design Principle

The brief should prioritize signal over noise.

It should help a product leader quickly understand what matters this week — not summarize everything that happened.
