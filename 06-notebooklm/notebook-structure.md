# NotebookLM Knowledge Layer

NotebookLM acts as the research and knowledge layer of the PM AI OS. It is where synthesized learning accumulates over time and becomes available as grounded context for AI-assisted PM work.

---

## Purpose

The NotebookLM layer exists to:

- store synthesized research and customer evidence
- provide grounded context for discovery, strategy, and execution work
- separate raw source material from structured PM analysis
- make accumulated knowledge reusable across initiatives

---

## Notebook Categories

### 1. Product Knowledge

Documents that define what the product is and how it works.

- PRDs and approved specs
- strategy docs and directional memos
- architecture notes and system context

### 2. Customer Voice

Synthesized customer evidence and qualitative signals.

- interview summaries and key themes
- support ticket patterns and recurring issues
- churn analysis and exit feedback

### 3. Analytics

Performance context and measurement documentation.

- metric definitions and instrumentation notes
- experiment summaries and outcomes
- dashboard references and data source notes

### 4. Market Intelligence

External context and competitive signals.

- competitor notes and capability comparisons
- industry research and trend summaries
- pricing analysis and positioning comparisons

### 5. Execution

Operational history and decision record.

- launch retros and post-mortems
- decision memos with rationale
- roadmap updates and sequencing changes

---

## Ingestion Rules

Add new material to NotebookLM when:

- new discovery research is completed
- a metrics analysis or experiment concludes
- a significant product decision is made
- new strategy context shifts priorities or direction

Keep the knowledge layer clean:

- do not store raw, unprocessed notes — synthesize first
- do not store confidential artifacts that should remain in company systems
- do not store personally identifiable customer information
- prefer summaries and structured extracts over full document dumps

---

## Usage pattern

Before starting a PM task, check the relevant notebook category for existing context before generating new analysis. The goal is to build on accumulated knowledge, not reinvent it.
